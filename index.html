This document serves as the official rulebook for the **Bengal Bash 5.0**. These rules are a complete set that combines the Bengal Bash Official Game Rules with the latest regulations from the International Cricket Council (ICC). All participants are expected to be familiar with this lawbook. **In the event of a conflict, the rules specifically mentioned in this lawbook will take precedence over standard ICC rules.** For any matter not covered herein, the decisions of the Tournament Committee, guided by the general principles of the ICC, will be final.

1. General Principles & Code of Conduct

All participants, including players and team officials, must uphold the **spirit of cricket**. Respect for umpires and their decisions is paramount. Any form of verbal abuse, aggressive behavior, or dissent towards a match official, opponent, or teammate is strictly prohibited and will be met with disciplinary action. Players and team officials are expected to maintain Religious values, discipline, and sportsmanship at all times.

2. Tournament Structure & Points System

The tournament will be played in a league format, followed by a playoff stage. A win in a league match will award **2 points**. A loss will award **0 points**. A tie or a no-result will award **1 point** to each team. The top four teams based on points at the end of the league phase will advance to the playoffs. If teams have equal points, their final position will be determined by **Net Run Rate (NRR)**.

2.1. Playoff/Knockout Match Tie-Breaker (Super Over)

In the event that a playoff match (Eliminator, Qualifier, or Final) ends in a tie, the winner will be determined by a **Super Over**, adhering to current ICC playing conditions.

  • A Super Over involves each team playing one additional over (6 balls). The team that batted second in the main match will bat first in the Super Over.
  • Each team nominates three batsmen and one bowler for the Super Over. A team's Super Over innings is over if they lose two wickets.
  • The team that scores the most runs in their Super Over is declared the winner.
  • If the Super Over is Also Tied: If the first Super Over also ends in a tie, subsequent Super Overs will be played until a winner is determined. The boundary countback rule will **not** be used.

3. Team & Player Registration

Each team must register a squad of a maximum of **13 players**. Only players who are officially registered and approved by the Tournament Committee are eligible to play in the tournament. A minimum of **8 players** is required for a team to start a match.

Player Eligibility 🇧🇩 🇳🇿:

The tournament is open only to players of **Bangladeshi origin**, regardless of their current country of residence. All players must be present in New Zealand on the day of the game.

4. Match Format & Timing

**League & Knockout Matches:** All games will consist of **8 overs** per side. The Final match can be extended to **10 overs** per side at the discretion of the Tournament Committee.

Matches must begin on their scheduled time. A grace period of 5 minutes will be granted, after which a team unable to field a minimum of 8 players will forfeit the match.

Time Limits and Over Rate

  • **Maximum Innings Duration (8 Overs):** Must be completed in **36 minutes**.
  • **Maximum Innings Duration (10 Overs - Final):** Must be completed in **45 minutes**.
  • **Maximum Over Rate:** This equates to a maximum of **4.5 minutes** per over. The penalty for slow over rates (one less fielder outside the circle) will be applied if the fielding team is not in position to bowl the final over within the allotted time.

Intervals and Field Management

STRICT TIME LIMIT

  • The match duration includes the **Toss**, **1st Innings**, and a strict **4-minute Innings Break**.
  • Teams must complete the **2nd Innings** and **exit the field completely** within the designated window.
  • This rule applies regardless of whether there is a back-to-back game.
  • There will be no excuses for delays.

Over Rate Penalty (Latest ICC Rule):

To curb slow over rates, if the fielding team is not in a position to bowl the first ball of the final over within the allotted time, they will be penalized. For the remainder of the innings, one less fielder will be allowed outside the 30-yard circle.

4.1. Match Scheduling & Delays

**Non-Start Rescheduling:** If any game **does not start at all** due to unforeseen circumstances (e.g., ground unavailability, extreme weather), it will be **rescheduled later on for one time only**. The Tournament Committee will not reschedule a game more than once.

**Abandonment Penalty:** If the rescheduled game is not played within its designated time, or if the initial non-start game is unable to be rescheduled a second time, the game will be treated as an **abandoned match** and **1 point will be awarded to both teams**.

5. Playing Conditions

5.1. Batting Rules

  • **No LBW:** There will be no LBW (Leg Before Wicket) dismissal in this tournament.
  • **Stance Monitoring:** The umpire will monitor the batsman's standing position and will warn a batsman who is deliberately obstructing the stumps.
  • **Penalty for Stance Violation:** After a verbal warning, if a batsman continues to stand in a position that obstructs the stumps, the umpire will deduct **5 runs** from the team's total. This deduction can be applied multiple times at the umpire's discretion.
  • **No Runners:** Runners for injured batsmen are not permitted under any circumstances.
  • **Free Hit:** A free hit will be awarded for all no-balls. The batsman cannot be dismissed on a free hit unless by run-out. All field placements and bowling restrictions remain the same for the free hit delivery.
  • **Latest Caught Out Rule:** If a batter is out caught, the new batter must come to the striker's end, regardless of whether the batters crossed while the ball is in the air.
  • Stumped: A batsman is out stumped if they step out of their crease to play a stroke, miss the ball, and the wicket-keeper breaks the wicket with the ball before the batsman can return.
  • Hit Wicket: A batsman is out hit wicket if they knock down their own wickets with their bat or any part of their person while playing a shot or setting off for a run.

5.2. Bowling Rules

  • **General Maximum:** Any bowler can bowl a maximum of **2 overs** per innings.
  • **No-Balls:** A no-ball will be called for any delivery that is over the batsman's waist on the full, or for overstepping the bowling crease. All no-balls result in a Free Hit.
  • **Wide:** A delivery is considered wide if it passes on either side of the batsman such that they are unable to hit it.
  • **Illegal Bowling Action (Chucking):** Any bowler suspected of chucking will be judged by the on-field umpire, whose decision will be final. The bowler will be warned, and subsequent violations may result in the bowler being removed from the attack for the remainder of the match. **If a bowler is confirmed by the umpire to have an illegal bowling action (chucking), the bowler will be restricted from bowling for the remainder of the tournament.**
  • **Mankad Rule:** In contrast to the current ICC rule, for this tournament, a bowler must provide a **single warning** to the non-striker before attempting a run-out under the Mankad rule. A subsequent attempt will result in the non-striker being run out.
  • **Saliva Ban:** The use of saliva to shine the ball is permanently banned. Players may only use sweat to maintain the ball.

5.3. Fielding Rules (PowerPlay)

  • **Duration:** The powerplay lasts for the first **3 overs** of the innings (standard for 10-over format).
  • **Fielding Restrictions:** During these first 3 overs, a maximum of only **two fielders** are allowed to be placed outside the 30-yard circle. After the initial 3 overs, the fielding restrictions change, and up to **five fielders** are allowed outside the inner circle.

6. Equipment Regulations

  • **Ball:** Only **tennis balls covered with white electrical tape** may be used. No other type of ball is authorized.
  • **Bats:** Fiber bats are strictly prohibited.
  • **Unauthorized Equipment:** If any batsman uses a fiber bat or any other unauthorized equipment, and it is inspected and confirmed by the Match Referee, the player's team will be penalized **30 runs**. The player will be banned for the entire tournament, and a stern warning will be issued to both the player and the team.

7. Player Availability & Substitutions

  • **Player Absence:** A player who is absent from the field for more than 8 minutes must spend an equivalent amount of time on the field before they are allowed to bowl or bat.
  • **Substitutions:** A substitute fielder may be used, but they are not permitted to bowl or bat. All substitutions must be approved by the umpires and the Tournament Committee prior to the start of the match.
  • **Concussion Substitutes (Latest ICC Rule):** In case of a concussion, a like-for-like replacement is allowed. The player diagnosed with a concussion must observe a mandatory seven-day rest period before returning to competitive play.

8. Scoring & Match Results

A match is decided by the team that scores the most runs.

  • **Tied Match:** If a match ends with both teams having the same score after the second innings is complete, a tie is declared. Both teams will be awarded with **1 point** each.
  • **Minimum Overs:** A minimum of **5 overs** must be bowled in each innings for a match to constitute a contest and for a result to be declared.
  • Rain-Affected Matches:
    • **Decision Authority:** **During or post-rain, the Match Referee will make the final decision with the advice of the On-Field Umpires on whether play is safe and possible.** No players or team management will be involved in making this decision.
    • **Suspended Match:** If play is stopped but there is a possibility of resuming later, often with revised overs, the match is declared **Suspended** (not abandoned).
    • **Revised Target Calculation (DLS Method):** If the match is shortened, the target for the second team (Team B) will be calculated using the official **Duckworth-Lewis-Stern (DLS) Method**. This method applies to **all scenarios** where overs are lost, including:
      • **Team A's Innings Interrupted (5-9 Overs):** If Team A's innings is cut short between the 5th and 9th over, the DLS calculation will compensate Team A for the loss of the final aggressive overs.
      **The Match Referee is solely responsible for obtaining and applying the correct DLS target.**
    • **Abandoned Match:** If the minimum number of overs cannot be completed in both innings, the match is **Abandoned**. It is then declared a "No Result," and both teams will be awarded **1 point** each.

9. Net Run Rate (NRR) Explained & Scenarios

Net Run Rate is a method of calculating a team's performance relative to other teams in a tournament. It is used as a tie-breaker when two or more teams finish with the same number of points.

NRR Formula:

NRR = (Total Runs Scored / Total Overs Faced) - (Total Runs Conceded / Total Overs Bowled)

(Team's Run Rate) - (Opponent's Run Rate)

A positive NRR indicates a team is scoring runs faster than their opponents are, while a negative NRR means the opposite.

Key NRR Rules for this Tournament:

  • **All-Out Rule:** If a team is bowled out (loses 10 wickets) in less than its full quota of overs, the calculation of its Net Run Rate will be based on the full quota of overs (e.g., **8.0 overs**) to which it was entitled, and **not** the number of overs in which the team was dismissed.
  • **Successful Chase Rule:** If a team successfully chases a target in less than the full quota, the **actual number of overs faced** is used for the calculation.

NRR Scenarios (Based on an 8-Over Match)

Scenario 1: Team Bats First and Wins (Full Quota Used)

Team Runs Scored Overs Faced Runs Conceded Overs Bowled Result
Team A 80 8.0 64 8.0 Wins by 16 runs

**Team A NRR:** (80 / 8.0) - (64 / 8.0) = 10.00 - 8.00 = +2.00

Scenario 2: Team Chases and Wins Quickly (Winning by Wickets)

Team Runs Scored Overs Faced Runs Conceded Overs Bowled Result
Team D (Batting 2nd) 82 6.2* 80 8.0 Wins by 7 wickets

*Note: 6.2 overs = 6.33 overs (decimal). Actual overs faced are used.

**Team D NRR:** (82 / 6.33) - (80 / 8.0) = 12.95 - 10.00 = +2.95

Scenario 3: Team is All-Out (Lost by Wickets)

Team Runs Scored Overs Faced Runs Conceded Overs Bowled Result
Team E (Batting 1st) 70 8.0** 72 7.2 Loses match

**\*\*Note:** Team E was all out in 7.0 overs, but for NRR calculation, the full **8.0 overs** are counted (Rule 1).

**Team E NRR:** (70 / 8.0) - (72 / 7.33) = 8.75 - 9.82 = -1.07

10. Behavior on the Field & Penalties

Players must not enter the field of play without the umpire's permission. If a player from one of the competing teams enters the field without permission due to anger, aggressive behavior, or with the intention to disrupt the game, a stern warning will be issued. A second, consecutive incident will result in a **5-run penalty** for the offending team.

Official Report & Severe Penalties

If an umpire reports any team or player for misbehavior, abuse, or serious behavioral issues, the **Tournament Committee** will review the incident. Upon validation of the report, the Committee will impose a mandatory **15-run punishment** on the offending team.

Maximum Sanctions:

For severe misconduct, the Tournament Committee reserves the right to enforce the following maximum penalties:

  • Immediate suspension of the player for the remainder of the tournament.
  • Suspension of the entire team from the current tournament.
  • A ban on the player or team from participating in the next Bengal Bash tournament as well.

All fielding concerns must be raised by the **team captain only**. For the batting team, only the striker or non-striker may speak to the umpire during the course of play. Any player or official found breaching these rules may face disciplinary action at the discretion of the Tournament Committee.

11. Tournament Committee & Umpire Authority

The **Tournament Committee** have the final authority to interpret, amend, and enforce these rules.

The appointed **umpires** have the final authority to interpret and enforce these rules on the field of play.

11.1. Match Referee & Committee Roles

Match Referee:

**Arifur Rahman** will serve as the official Match Referee for the tournament and will be the final authority on the continuation of play in rain-affected matches.

12. Acknowledgment & Agreement

By participating in the Bengal Bash 5.0, every player and team owner confirms that they have read, understood, and agreed to abide by these rules. They further agree that the Tournament Committee’s decisions will be final in all matters.
